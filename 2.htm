Step-by-step Instructions

Step 1

Place the compass on one end of the line segment.

Step 2

Set the compass width to a approximately two thirds the line length. The actual width does not matter.

Step 3

Without changing the compass width, draw a circle.

Step 4

Again without changing the compass width, place the compass point on the the other end of the line. Draw an a circle so that the arcs cross the first circle.

Step 5

Using a straightedge, draw a line between the points where the circles intersect.

Step 6

This line is perpendicular to the first line and bisects it (cuts it at the exact midpoint of the line).
