Constructing a 30° Angle

 

This demonstration shows how to construct a 30° angle from scratch using a compass and straightedge.

Step-by-step Instructions

Step 1

Draw a line segment which will become one side of the angle. (Skip this step if you are given this line.) The exact length is not important. Label it AD. A will be the angle's vertex.

Step 2

Set the compass on A, and set its width to any convenient setting.

Step 3

Draw a circle across AD. Label the point where it crosses PQ as point B.

Step 4

Without changing the compass width, move the compass to the point S. Draw a circle that crosses the first one and goes well to the right. Label the point where the two circles cross as point E.

Step 5

Without changing the compass width, move the compass to the point E, and draw an arc across the previous arc, creating point C.

Step 6

Draw a line from A to C.

Step 7

Done. The angle DAC has a measure of 30°
